Wuxi is a city in China not far from Shanghai. It is the second largest city in the Jiangsu Province and is sometimes known as Little Shanghai and has a thriving economy. There are beautiful parks to visit and a bustling downtown area with plenty of bars to get lost in and amazing street food to try. Hot pot is popular and itâ€™s a great way to socialise with friends as well as get a bite to eat. Although it can be cold in the winter, it starts warming up rather quickly, usually around April. One of the biggest festivals celebrated in China is the Dragon Boat Festival. People flock to see the dragon boat races and eat Zongzi, which is rice wrapped in bamboo to form a pyramid shape. It is celebrated on the fifth day of the fifth month of the Chinese Lunisolar calendar and commemorates the life and death of the scholar Qu Yuan.
Beijing is the capital of China and is the second largest city in China, with over twenty-one million residents. It is a busy and exciting place to explore, with lots of museums to visit. It is one of the oldest cities in the world, its history dating back over three millennia. Beijing is the place to be to celebrate the Spring Festival, the celebration of New Year in China. Celebrations can last for fifteen days! Each new year is marked by one of the twelve animals in the Chinese Zodiac. The year 2023 is the Year of the Rabbit.
